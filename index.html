About Event

Exploring AI & Cybersecurity: From Attack Prevention to AI Solutions

This event is designed to provoke thought and ignite debate among the bright minds in attendance. The symposium will explore the multifaceted relationship between artificial intelligence (AI) and cybersecurity, as well as the fields themselves. Organised by Master's students specialising in AI and Cybersecurity, this event aims to showcase the latest advances and the crucial interplay between these two dynamic fields.

Meet Speakers Check out Schedule

AI and cybersecurity in one? Great! The showcase aims to present the evolving landscape of AI and cybersecurity. It will explore AI solutions to critical problems such as configuring large-scale electronic systems, detecting counterfeit Naira notes, and optimising search. The symposium will explore how AI is being used to develop sophisticated attack prevention mechanisms, detect and respond to threats in real time, and create novel cybersecurity solutions.

  • Investigating the Grounding Bottleneck for a Large-Scale Configuration Problem: Existing Tools and Constraint-Aware Guessing

    Speaker Veronika Semmelrock
    16:00 - 16:15
    BBB-classroom
    Download Slides Download Code
    In recent years, Answer Set Programming (ASP) has become an essential tool for solving complex configuration problems across various domains. One very important domain is the configuration of large-scale electronic systems, however, a fundamental challenge in applying ASP to large-scale problems is the grounding bottleneck. We will investigate state-of-the-art ASP systems and their limitations and propose a new approach called CAG (Constraint-Aware Guessing).
